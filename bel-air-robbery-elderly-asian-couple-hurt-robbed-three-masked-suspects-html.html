Bel Air robbery: Elderly Asian couple hurt, robbed by three masked suspects

An elderly Asian couple was tied up and robbed inside their million-dollar Bel Air home before the suspects got away with their safe and wiped down surfaces with bleach to cover their tracks. 

The incident happened around 1am Saturday in the 1300 block of Casiano Road in the ritzy Los Angeles suburb when the suspects reportedly rushed in as the couple was watching television. 

The elderly husband was reportedly struck several times in the head with an unidentified object while the woman was restrained. 

'They put a bag over her head and they went in for four hours and they bleached everything for fingerprints,' neighbor Amy Rahbar told KTLA of the thieves. 

Police are currently searching for the suspects who terrorized the elderly duo and asked the public for help to identify the culprits.

Police were called to the scene for reports of a robbery in progress and arrived to find the couple, described as a man in his 70s and a woman in her 60s, injured. 

According to Rahbar, who spoke with the couple's son, the thieves seized a vulnerable moment when the home's door was reportedly open. 

'I did meet their son this morning and he was very concerned and upset,' the neighbor told the outlet  Saturday.  

'His mom was watching TV and she had the door cracked open,' Rahbar said. 

The house is located in expensive Bel Air, where homes on the same block can cost several million dollars. It's unknown how much money was taken from the victims.

It's unclear at this time if the couple was the target for the thieves or if the thieves were looking for any potential cash grabs in the area. 

Rahbar said she knows the couple's small grandchildren often stay at the house but they were not inside at the time of the robbery.  

'They're lovely neighbors and it's unfortunate to hear what's going on,' she said. 

The couple sustained non-life-threatening injuries and were not taken to the hospital. 

The suspects who bleached the home on their way out remain at large as police search for surveillance footage from the neighborhood. 

Police are currently asking that anyone who may have information on the incident reach out immediately at 877-527-3247. 

According to Zillow, the upscale home in Bel Air is just over 3,700 square feet and has four bedrooms and three bathrooms.
