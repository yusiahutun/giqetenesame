Frequently Asked Questions

What Ingredients Should You Look for in a Moisturizer if You Have Dry Skin?

"The main ingredients to look for include lipids like fatty acids, ceramides, and hyaluronic acid, a.k.a. dry skin’s best friend," says Brauer. Also, if you're looking to combine hydration with anti-aging benefits, "many moisturizers also include antioxidants that help to protect skin from oxidative stress, which contributes to fine lines and wrinkles."

How Should You Layer Your Products?

In the simplest version of your skincare routine, you'll cleanse, then apply serums or treatments, and then moisturize. Moisturizer is especially important when using certain actives, which can further dry out your skin.

"If using products like retinol, it is important to use a rich hydrated moisturizer to counteract the potentially drying factor of the retinol," says Brauer. "Stay away from products that have salicylic acid, as the exfoliating formula can still strip the skin of its moisture."
