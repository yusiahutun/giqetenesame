Is Midland's 'Make a Little' a Hit? [Listen]

"But Midland, what is 'Make a Little' really about?" said no one ever. The trio's newest retro swinger is not just a sonic throwback. It recalls the wink, wink, WINK! that so many country songs from a lost generation embraced.

Singer Mark Wystrach spends most of "Make a Little" hinting at his intentions before just flat-out saying, "I’m talkin’ ‘bout lovin’ all night" as if it needed to be spelled out. The three-minute-long country charmer will keep you smiling. A more skeptical review may point out that once upon a time, country males showed some effort in trying to get a girl into the bedroom and that these days "Hey babe" and "Dayum!" are the best a gentleman can come up with. Don't go there. Enjoy "Make a Little" for what it is: Alabama reincarnated.

Perhaps most important to this group's future is that "Make a Little" proves a dedication to a more vintage style is not creatively restricting. One hears Dwight Yoakam's influence throughout a track that's nothing like "Drinkin' Problem." Versatility won't be an issue for Midland.
