Best bridal lingerie and underwear for your wedding day

Whether it's the dress, the veil, the shoes or the jewellery, choosing what to wear on your wedding day often involves plenty of research into finding out exactly what you want – and this goes for the underwear too. In fact, it can feel like a bit of minefield when trying to work out what you need to wear underneath your dress.

Whether you are trying to complement a tricky neckline, are thinking that this might be a good opportunity to try a new style for the first time or just have absolutely no idea where to even start looking, we are here to help.

Below, we speak to some of the best bridal lingerie experts in the business to solve the most common queries around wedding underwear.

I'm wearing a strapless dress, should I wear a bustier?

There are no fixed rules when it comes to choosing what to wear beneath your wedding dress, but bustiers offer coverage without the full-on squeeze of a corset, so they're a good solution if you want something more exciting than a standard bra and pants set, but don't want to feel too sucked-in all day.

However, if a full bustier still feels too claustrophobic (once your dress is on top, you will feel more fastened-in than usual) then try a long-line bra with removable straps – perfect under a dress with full décolleté. Ultimately, it is all about you feeling your most comfortable self, as Bale Sekulceska, from the La Perla Sloane Street Store tells us.

"Our advice for brides-to-be on their special day is to find something that you feel your most beautiful in, but equally your most comfortable in. La Perla’s corsets, bustier and bodysuits, especially from our Maison Contouring Shapewear line, are designed to emphasise the natural contours of a woman’s body, allowing the bride to feel her most empowered without feeling constricted.

"They work seamlessly underneath a bridal gown and are a popular choice with our customers. Alternatively, an exquisitely crafted strapless bra like our Petit Macrame style can also give you the support that you need under a strapless dress while making you feel amazing from the inside out. We’ve found that the lingerie our customers choose to wear is just as important to them as the exterior bridal outfit itself."

I've never worn a corset, where do I even start?

When it comes to corsetry, the key is seeking the right advice and being meticulous when it comes to sizing. "Wearing the correct size for your shape is so important; if anything is too big or too tight, it can be really unflattering," says Sarah Shotton, creative director at Agent Provocateur. "It's important to get properly fitted every time you buy new lingerie, especially a corset, as your size can depend on the brand," explains Shotton.

Agent Provocateur has a really strong technical team that focuses on fit, combined with beautiful fabrics, so that nothing feels too structural or utilitarian. "All of our bridal corsets and sets can be worn during and after the wedding as they enhance the female form to look its best while remaining beautiful," says Shotton. A popular choice is the Mercy corset, £395, which has padded cups and rigid fabric, nipping in the waist for an hourglass look.

Is it a good idea to have two sets of underwear – a practical set for during the day and more romantic lingerie to change into for my wedding night?

If you have a tricky dress that requires sturdy shapewear or unflattering underwear wizardry such as silicone straps, you might want to consider having a second set of pretty underwear to change into when the party is over. Get the day-time girders sorted first by choosing reliable shapewear.

"No VPL finishes on knickers and shapewear will ensure no ugly lines digging in under close-fitting dresses," says Soozie Jenkinson, head of design at Marks & Spencer. "Choosing lingerie with minimal seams, such as our innovative Smoothlines bras, will be smooth under clothing, and if you need firm support, sculpting pieces such as our WaistSculpt 'wear your own bra-slip' allows you to create an hourglass silhouette under close-fitting dresses." Marks & Spencer also has a handy online bra fitting tool, which helps with accurate measuring even if you don't have time to go into a store for a fitting.

When it comes to finding your wedding night underwear, the process should be a lot simpler. "Your wedding night brings an opportunity to indulge in ultra-feminine lingerie and romantic boudoir pieces, like silk and lace collections that make beautiful trousseau sets," says Jenkinson. If your budget is limited, then Marks & Spencer's collaboration with Rosie Huntington-Whiteley has vintage-inspired French-designed lace bras, knickers and teddies that are affordable but feel luxurious.

I don't tend to wear suspenders, are they just too much of a faff for my wedding day?

"If you're thinking of wearing stockings, suspenders make a beautiful addition to the look, but this is an option you need to try with your dress in advance," explains Charlotte McKee, the lingerie stylist at Rigby & Peller. "The suspender belt should sit above your hips on your waist to avoid any uncomfortable pulling or slipping of items throughout the day." Not sure what goes on top of what? "The belt should be worn first, underneath your briefs, for extra ease and shouldn't feel too tight. This will help to reduce the visibility of straps sticking out through your dress when you sit down," says McKee.

She recommends that you opt for stockings as opposed to hold-ups, which can sometimes be problematic to thread through the belt loops due to their silicone piping. "Make sure you practice a few times before the big day by wearing your chosen suspender belt and stockings around the house," she advises. "You should also avoid straps that are too short and opt, where possible, for adjustable versions so that you can work out the correct fit and size of stocking most comfortable for you well in advance of the big day." One final tip – always have at least one pair of spare stockings on the day in case of emergency ladders.
