Nine WWE babes who stripped naked for Playboy - from Chyna to Sable

WWE and Playboy are two of the most recognisable entertainment brands in the world.

So perhaps it's no surprise that they've jumped into bed together on more than one occasion over the years.

No, the late, great Hugh Hefner, founder of Playboy, never completed an RKO on Randy Orton, as iconic as that would have been.

But some of WWE's hottest babes did go the other way and appear in the Lothario's iconic magazine.

Maybe it shouldn't be much of a shock given that wrestling is a popular pastime in most bedrooms up and down the country.

With that in mind, Daily Star Sport takes a look at nine WWE hotties who stripped naked for Playboy.

Maryse

Blonde bombshell Maryse Mizanin also began her career as a beauty pageant contest, winning the coveted title of Miss Hawaiian Tropic Canada in 2003.

She joined WWE three years later and appeared in Playboy no fewer than 12 times according to IMBd, with Playboy Vixens shoot selling out in less than 24 hours on the east coast.

Maryse was also on the cover of Playboy's 2007 Girls of Canada calendar, and she even has her own reality TV show entitled Miz and Mrs, based on her relationship with husband Mike.

Former Miss Galaxy winner Torrie Wilson first stripped off for Playboy in 2003, helping to develop a feud with fellow wrestler Nidia Guenard.

She then appeared on the cover alongside another WWE stunner, Sable (more on her later), sparking a row with Raw Divas Stacy Keibler and Miss Jackie.

After weeks of bitter rivalry, Wilson and Sable defeated Keibler and Miss Jackie in a quite wonderfully titled Playboy Evening Gown match at WrestleMania XX by stripping their opponents of their evening gowns. How tasteful.

Unlike most of the girls on this list, Carmella DeCesare bared all for Playboy before she made the transition to WWE.

DeCesare came to the magazine's attention after she applied to a Fox reality show called Who Wants to Be a Playboy Centerfold.

Sure enough, she was chosen as a Playboy Cyber Girl of the Week, then named Cyber Girl of the Month before earning the coveted title of Playboy's Miss April in 2003.

She later won a short WWE contract and went on to feature in arguably the sporting event of the 2000s, a Lingerie Pillow Fight on Taboo Tuesday. We don't seem to remember that featuring on Grandstand.

Ashley Massaro

Hired as part of WWE's Diva Search programme, Ashley Massaro spent her formative years in the sport participating in an array of Bra and Panties contests.

She then migrated to SmackDown, where she experienced more of the same, and in February 2007 she appeared on the front cover of Playboy for the first time.

Not long after she also appeared in the Playboy Bunnymania Lumberjack match at WrestleMania XXIV, but ended up on the losing side against Beth Phoenix and Melina.

Christy Hemme

Christy Hemme's appearance on the front cover of Playboy has an unusual backstory.

Before accepting the iconic gig, she had to get the permission of her dad, who requested that she never pose nude.

Thankfully for her at least, he said yes, and Hemme went au naturel for the April 2005 edition of the magazine.

She also competed at quarterback for the Chicago Bliss Lingerie Football League team in the 2006 Lingerie Bowl.

Maria Kanellis

WWE's Diva Search really does have a lot to answer for. Maria Kanellis, a star of beauty pageants in her youth, was also drafted via the Diva route and has enjoyed a long career in the sport ever since.

Kanellis was reportedly first asked to pose for Playboy when she was just 19, but turned down the offer for fear of embarrassing her sister.

Those fears subsided some years later, however, when she posed for the cover of the April 2008 issue, with an accompanying nude pictorial. Later that year, FHM named her 53rd on their "The Sexiest Women 2008" list.

Candice Michelle


Having started her career as a model and an actor, Candice Michelle was hired by WWE in 2004.

It didn't take long for her to follow the traditional Diva route of posing for Playboy, showing herself off on the cover and starring in a nude pictorial in April 2006.

The first former Diva Search contestant to win a WWE title, she was released from her contract in 2009 but returned in 2019 to win the WWE 24/7 championship.

During her time in WWE, she also appeared in several intriguingly named contests, including the first ever Bra and Panties Gauntlet Match and the Playboy Pillow Fight at Wrestlemania 22.

Chyna

Billed as the Ninth Wonder of the World during her time with WWF (later WWE), Chyna, real name Joan Laurer, was a a firm favourite among wrestling and Playboy fans alike.

She appeared in the magazine twice, with her first shoot one of the best selling issues of all-time. An unlikely sex symbol, she released a sex tape with fellow wrestler Sean Walman, entitled 1 Night in China, in 2004.

Chyna appeared in further videos, including Backdoor to Chyna, which somehow missed out on an Oscar nomination, and was widely referred to as the First Lady of Sports Entertainment.

Sable

Don't mess with Sable, because you'll have iconic former WWE, UFC and NCAA heavyweight champion Brock Lesnar to answer to.

The couple, who met while working for WWE, have been married since 2005.

Sable first broke onto the wrestling scene in the later 1990s and was arguably as crucial to the Attitude era as household names like Steve Austin and The Rock.

She holds the distinction of being the first WWE babe to strip nude for Playboy, while her appearance on the front cover of the April 1999 issue made it one of the highest selling editions ever.

A stone cold sex symbol, she appeared on the front cover of the mag three times.
