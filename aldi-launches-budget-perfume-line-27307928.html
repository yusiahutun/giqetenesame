Aldi launches budget perfume line including Thierry Mugler Angel dupe for under 6

Budget supermarket Aldi has released some new beauty products this week – and you can now bag a Thierry Mugler Angel dupe for under £6.

Aldi’s Health & Beauty team have been working hard to develop a host of new products, which land in stores on Thursday, June 23.

There are three new perfumes up for grabs, as well as some new skincare and tanning products.

The goodies are said to be excellent (and much cheaper) versions of popular high-end brands.

Some of the beauty special buys will see customers save up to 95% when compared to similar brands.

What's on offer?

Lacura Luxe Eau de Parfum (£5.99, 100ml)

The fragrance boasts notes of saffron and iris that complement undertones of papyrus and heady vetiver.

It's been compared to Marc Jacobs's Decadence , which comes in at the retail price of £54.99 for 100ml.

The product description says it's "deeply woody and sensual" with "creamy notes of jasmine."

Lacura Guardian Eau de Parfum (£5.99, 100ml)

This particular scent has been compared to Thierry Mugler's Angel, which will set you back £116.00 for 100ml.

The fragrance is said to be "carefully formulated with sweet scents of pineapple, coconut and mandarin orange."

There are also additional base notes of vanilla, amber, caramel and Tonka bean.

Lacura Floral Love Eau de Parfum (£5.99, 100ml)

The scent described as "a celebration of all things floral" is said to be similar to Viktor & Rolf's Flowerbomb, which is priced at £105 per 100ml.

According to the product description there are "notes of rose, freesia and African orange flower, complemented by darker, woody aromas of bergamot, musk, patchouli, and creamy vanilla."

It's described as a sophisticated scent for those who love floral fragrances.

What else is new?

Vibrating Rose Quartz Sculpting Roller (£12.99)


Aldi also said it is taking its "must have" skincare tool to the next level by launching it's own Vibrating Rose Quartz Sculpting Roller.

It's one of the latest products to be released by the store's health and beauty brand, Lacura.

Face rolling is said to work to alleviate tension in overworked muscles and also helps to de-puff, tone and lift complexions.

The rose quartz roller helps to shift toxins and blockages, which will leave your cheekbones appearing higher and sharper.

The budget store's version of the beauty tool is battery powered, and also uses sonic vibrations to soothe tension, stimulate blood circulation and boost collagen levels.

This could help to leave skin looking and feeling radiant, and it's been designed to help shoppers achieve results usually created by spa facials but for a fraction of the price.

At £12.99 beauty lovers will save up to 35% when compared to if they splashed the cash on JML’s Vibrating Facial Roller and Massager.

Luxury Illuminating Tan Drops

Tanning fanatics will also be glad to hear about the return of Lacura’s luxurious and five-star rated Luxury Illuminating Tan Drops (£4.99, 28ml).

When the product first hit shelves last Christmas it sold out within weeks, but now it's back on the market.

Boasting a hydrating and lightweight formula, Aldi’s luxe drops are perfect for achieving a golden, sun kissed tan while also minimising uneven skin tones and imperfections for a natural glow.

The product description says it's "equipped with a pipette dispenser" so beauty lovers can have "ultimate control over application for a fuss and streak free finish."

Many shoppers have also raved about how good it is online, with one having commented: "I love this product.

"One drop in my moisturiser provides a lovely healthy glow.

"I usually use Tan Luxe products which are expensive in comparison. I will be swapping to Aldi."
