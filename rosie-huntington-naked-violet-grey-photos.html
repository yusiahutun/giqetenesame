Rosie Huntington-Whiteley Poses Totally Naked For Violet Grey -- See The Sexy Shots!

If you've got it, flaunt it!

Rosie Huntington-Whiteley bares her bod in a new photo spread for Violet Grey, The Violet Files magazine.

In the sexy shots, the 27-year-old poses completely nude with just her arms and legs strategically placed to preserve her modesty. And in a few other photos, the blonde beauty rocks an S&M-inspired corset and bikini top.

Rosie likewise opens up about the first time she ever stripped down to her birthday suit in the accompanying interview with the mag.

"I had just started modeling," she explains. "I was in London, and it was a different time. I honestly think I was a lot more free when I was younger. Kate Moss was my favorite supermodel; she set the standard in terms of nudity as fashion and art. I think being comfortable in one's skin is the most attractive quality a girl can have. Beauty for me is about a good workout routine — that and a signature scent, preferably one that smells like a garden rose."

The model also says that she likes to surround herself with people who inspire her. "I believe who you surround yourself with is a reflection of yourself. There's a quote I have always liked by Will Rogers: 'It takes a lifetime to build a good reputation, but you can lose it in a minute,'" she tells the mag.

And when it comes to Whiteley's sexy bod, she says it's all about finding the right balance.

"I don't want to be that miserable girl pushing a salad around my plate. At home, I eat clean, organic, simple. And when I go out with friends, I enjoy myself…I couldn't live life just restrictive all the time. It's just no fun to me. That's not a way to live. It's all balance," she confesses.

While the model already has successful lingerie and fragrance lines, she's got a few more things up her sleeves these days.

"Being a model, being an actress—it's not decided by you. You're chosen. You're cast, and that's fantastic, of course. There is always a sense of pride when you make the cut, but there has always been a big part of me that wants to make things happen for myself," she says. "I'd like to get myself into a position where I'm starting something on my own. That's my focus for this year."
