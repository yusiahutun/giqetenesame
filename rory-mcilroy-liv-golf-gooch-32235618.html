Rory McIlroy leaves door open to LIV Golf move as he hits back at Talor Gooch

Rory McIlroy has refused to rule out a shock move to LIV Golf as he hit back at Talor Gooch for his Masters dig.

But the Ulsterman also said he could retire at the age of 34 if he finally wins in Augusta in April. McIlroy’s former manager Chubby Chandler last week claimed “there's a 10 per cent possibility” that the world No.2 will follow Jon Rahm and join the Saudi-backed league in a mega-money deal.

The four-time Major champion admitted he felt like a “sacrificial lamb” for defending the PGA Tour after the framework agreement with PIF was announced last June. Before teeing up in the Cognizant Classic in Florida ton Thursday, McIlroy was asked about Chandler’s comments.

“I think he's writing a book, so there is that,” he smiled. “I spoke to Chubby in the Middle East at the start of the year. Never know. He might know a few things. Who knows?”

McIlroy was told Chandler had said there was a “good chance” he was going before saying “a 10% possibility”. Asked the real percentage, McIlroy said playfully: “Somewhere in the middle maybe. Who knows?”

But he could not resist having a go back at LIV star Talor Gooch who claimed earlier this week that McIlroy winning the Masters to complete his career Grand Slam would have an “asterisk” because many LIV stars will not be eligible to play in Georgia.

Augusta National has granted LIV’s Joaquin Niemann an invitation and McIlroy said: “Joaquin Niemann got an invite and I played with him a few weeks ago in Dubai, and he went down to Australia and won. He was in Oman last week. He has been chasing his tail around the world to get this, play his way into August or show enough form to warrant an invite. I don't know if the same can be said for Talor.”

The Ulsterman, 34, was in a relaxed mood near home in Florida as he reflected on his career which started in 2007. Asked if he could sustain the same hunger for the next 17 years, he said: “For the next 17, no. I would say for the next ten, absolutely. Or a green jacket and just walk away.”
