'People actually got sick from the odor': Kentucky coroner left a body in a hot van for a day instea

The brother of a dead man has accused a coroner of ruining his family's chance to say goodbye to their loved one by leaving his body in a hot van for an entire day instead of storing it in a freezer. 

Basill Peyton says that former coroner Raymond Vancleave, who used to work for Morgan County in Kentucky, ruined the sendoff for his brother, Nathan Peyton as the stench of his decomposing body made people sick.   

'When they buried him, he had to remain in the body bag. Closed casket,' Basill told KWTX. 

'People actually got sick from the odor that was emanating from the casket.' 

Basill is currently seeking unspecified damages from Vancleave and six other current and former Morgan County officials for the way the former coroner handled his brother's body. 

Former Morgan County coroner Raymond Vancleave is accused of negligently leaving a dead body in his van on a hot day 

Vancleave allegedly kept the body of Nathan Peyton (pictured) in a hot van for at least a day

Basill Peyton (pictured) is suing Vancleave and six others over allegations of negligence

He says that though he found his brother's body in a good condition, it degraded once it was in Raymond's hands.

The lawsuit claims that 'coroner Vancleave kept possession of the body of Nathan Peyton who was located in a sealed black body bag inside [his] SUV' that was parked throughout the day and night of the 'unseasonably' warm day Nathan passed away in December 2022. 

The lawsuit also claims that Vancleave did not have access to any morgues, freezer or cooling systems at any nearby facilities because his privileges were suspended as he failed to remove bodies in a timely manner. 

As a result, Basill's lawyers claim, Nathan's body quickly decomposed, causing a 'pungent and putrid' smell to come from his body.

The body was allegedly in such a state that Magoffin County Funeral Home was unable to embalm the body, and Nathan's loved ones were forced to go through with a closed-casket funeral.  

'Multiple pall bearers, visitors and funeral home staff vomited and became ill because of the foul and pungent odor coming from Mr. Peyton's coffin,' the lawsuit added. 

Though Basill places most of the blame on Vancleave, he also accuses six other Morgan County officials of being complicit in the incident, claiming they should have known about his alleged incompetence. 

The lawsuit says that the other Morgan Country officials should have known about Vancleave's allegedly 'negligent, reckless and careless' behaviour.

'There's not a day that goes by that I don't think of him,' Basill told local news outlet Lex18.

'That is something that will stay with us until the day we die, knowing how he was mistreated.' 

'It infuriates me that someone had to know his actions, if they didn't know, they should have known.'

'He was my only brother, I loved him more than anything. You just can't replace a brother.' 

Don McFarland, a lawyer representing Basill, told MailOnline: 

'We are not saying that Mr. Vancleave or any former or current member of the Morgan Fiscal Court are bad people. We are alleging that a mistake was made when it came to the disgusting treatment that occurred to the corpse of Nathan Peyton.

'This abuse was negligent, careless and in my opinion was reckless. In 2023, no human being should have been treated the way that Nathan Peyton was treated. 

'He should have been treated with respect and honor and his family, especially his brother Basill Peyton should have been able to properly say farewell to their loved one is a dignified manner. 

'This whole incident has been a nightmare for the Peyton family and Basil Peyton, who is a military veteran and now seeks psychiatric treatment at the VA medical hospital due to the trauma. 

'We simply do not want any family of Morgan County to have go through what the Peyton family has been made to endure. Please try to imagine how would you or any human being feel, if something this deplorable happened to someone that you love.'

MailOnline has contacted a lawyer representing Raymond Vancleave and the other Morgan County officials for comment.
