Biography

Gaylord Perry was born in Willamston, NC on September 15, 1938. Hall of fame pitcher known for his spitballs. He spent 22 years in the majors, winning 314 games, striking out 3,534 batters and posting a career 3.11 ERA. He hit his career first home run an hour after Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed on the moon. A friend of his father’s died while having his teeth pulled, and he became his namesake.
