Andrew J Marsico pleads no contest to misdemeanor charges in groping incident

A lobbyist married to an ABC reporter pleaded no contest to misdemeanor charges in a 2014 groping incident of a 27-year-old woman.

Andrew J Marsico, 40, of South Penn Square, Philadelphia, was sentenced to five years’ probation under a plea deal on Friday.

Marsico, who married 6ABC reporter Annie McCormick last year, was arrested in September 2014 for an incident in which he allegedly groped the woman while they were alone in his office at Greenlee Partners at about 10pm on August 21, 2014.

Andrew J Marsico (left), 40, of South Penn Square, Philadelphia, pleaded no contest on Friday to misdemeanor charges in a 2014 groping incident of a 27-year-old woman. Marsico married 6ABC reporter Annie McCormick (right) last year

Police say Marsico and the young woman went to his office at Bellevue, (pictured) apparently for her to have a job interview and the alleged assault happened inside his office at 10pm

According to police, the alleged victim met Marsico at an event at the Union League, a private members club in Philadelphia, reports CBS Philadelphia.

Police say the two then left and went to Marsico's office at the Bellevue, apparently for the young woman to have a job interview.

While alone, Marsico pushed the woman ‘against the wall at least two times, and at one time groped her, causing her pain’, assistant district Attorney Adam Geer said.

Geer also said that the woman ‘struck her head’ when she was pushed against the wall, according to Philly.com.

Marsico entered no-contest pleas on three misdemeanor charges, including recklessly endangering another person, simple assault and harassment. He did not comment before the judge.

Marsico and his journalist wife were married by former Pennsylvania Gov Ed Rendell last year.

After Marsico’s hearing, McCormick said of her husband’s case: ‘It's bulls***. You have to weigh whether you're going to trial and put your lives in 12 people's hands... There are choices to make.’

She said that her husband ‘is innocent of everything’, adding that when police came to arrest Marsico at their apartment at the Residences at the Ritz-Carlton at 3am, they ‘did no investigation’.

Marsico's wife (right) said that her husband ‘is innocent of everything’ and that police 'did no investigation' when they arrested him last year

Marsico was arrested in September 2014 for an incident in which he allegedly groped the woman while they were alone in his office at Greenlee Partners at about 10pm on August 21, 2014.

Marsico, pictured here with McCormick, entered no-contest pleas on three misdemeanor charges on Friday, including recklessly endangering another person, simple assault and harassment

Marsico was arrested a month after the incident and posted bail.

Marsico is also the brother of Dauphin County District Attorney Ed Marsico and a cousin, Ron Marsico, is a Republican state representative from the Harrisburg suburbs.

According to his LinkedIn page, he claims to still be employed by Greenlee Partners, one of the biggest lobbying firms in Harrisburg. But the company’s website has no mention of him.

Late on Friday McCormick released a statement via email on behalf of her husband: 'Today the Philadelphia District Attorney's Office decided to drop all sexual assault charges against me.’

The statement emphasized that though Marsico did not contest to three charges, ‘I am not admitting any guilt whatsoever as it relates to them’.

He said he ‘was taken out of my apartment in the middle of the night [by police] and held in custody for 17 hours. Prior to my arrest, they never attempted to come to my office or home.

‘Police never obtained video from my office (the alleged scene of the crime) which would have exonerated me.

‘I wanted the entire truth to come out at trial but, after much thought my family and I decided to accept the plea.’

According to police, the alleged victim met Marsico at an event at the Union League (pictured), a private members club in Philadelphia

He claimed that his accuser was not credible, but the ‘consequences of a jury trial could put me in jail, make me a Megan's Law Offender, and take away my law license even though I am an innocent man’.

District Attorney Office spokesman Cameron Kline called Marsico’s statement ‘disappointing’.

He said of the statement: ‘It is disappointing that the defendant is not accepting responsibility for the very crimes he conceded committing this afternoon in court.

‘We applaud the courage of the victim in coming forward to disclose the defendant's criminal conduct. As a result of his sentence of five years supervised probation, we believe it will adequately protect the safety of other young women in this commonwealth.’

In exchange for the plea, a felony charge of aggravated assault without consent against Marsico was dropped. Additionally, misdemeanor charges of unlawful restraint, false imprisonment and indecent assault were dropped.

Marsico was issued a stay-away order and was ordered to pay about $1,100 in restitution to the state’s victims compensation fund, which will reimburse the victim for hospital and transportation costs.

When Geer asked the judge for restitution, McCormick said ‘Jesus Christ!’ as she sat in the courtroom gallery.

The victim, who is now 28, was not in the courtroom on Friday. She did, however, testify in a preliminary hearing last year.
