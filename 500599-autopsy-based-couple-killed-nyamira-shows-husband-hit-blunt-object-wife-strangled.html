Autopsy on US-Based Couple Killed in Nyamira Shows Husband Was Hit With Blunt Object, Wife Strangled

  • Edward Morema and his wife Grace Mong'ina Morema were murdered by unknown assailants in their Nyamakoroto home
  • Edward Morema was hit two times on his head with a blunt object and sustained three stab injuries on his ribs
  • On the hand, Grace Morema died out of strangulation before the culprits dismantled the CCTV DVR from the house

Detectives in Nyamira county have launched a probe into the murder of a US-based couple.

The family of Edward Morema and his wife Grace Mong'ina Morema who were murdered by unknown assailants in their Nyamakoroto home is seeking a speedy investigation to bring the culprits to book.

According to the autopsy report conducted on the two bodies at Kisii Teaching and Referral hospital, Edward was hit two times on his head with a blunt object and sustained three stab injuries on his ribs while his wife Grace Morema died out of strangulation.

Masaba North sub-County police commander Robert Ndambiri said officers are still holding two workers in the homestead, who is helping with investigations.

The Morema family, which is yet to start funeral arrangements, described the couple as being supportive to the community when it came to school and church affairs as well as helping the needy.

Sad state of affairs

TUKO.co.ke understands that the news of the brutal murder of the couple hit Kenyans like a shock wave a few days ago.

This was after their bodies were discovered in their house on Tuesday, March 21.

Evidence indicated that the couple was brutally murdered by individuals who are yet to be identified.

As investigations into the homicide continue, officers from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations have indicated that the deaths could be related to marital woes.

Ruai land

According to information from one of the family members, the deceased professor and his wife had recently acquired a parcel of land in Ruai.

The relative who spoke anonymously indicated that they suspect the land could be the reason behind the deaths because it had been a topic of discussion for a while.

Masaba North Sub-County Police boss Robert Ndambiri told the media that detectives were trying to establish how the attackers got into their house because there were no signs of forced entry.

“We have arrested two people as we commence our investigations. They are a housemaid who used to live with the deceased in their single-storey house and a farm hand,” he said, as reported by Daily Nation.
