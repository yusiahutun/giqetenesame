Victoria Beckham Beauty Priming Moisturizer Review- Augustinus Bader for VB Skincare

Victoria Beckham teased that skincare was coming from her beauty line Victoria Beckham Beauty, but we never expected the first product to be a collab with biomedical scientist, Professor Augustinus Bader. His line of face and body creams have a cult-like following for their ability to make skin look totally rejuvenated. Their collaboration, called the Cell Rejuvenating Priming Moisturizer, is a lightweight but ultra-hydrating cream with a golden, shimmery finish that can be worn alone or under your favorite foundation.

"It's been a dream to develop, with Augustinus, a priming moisturizer that works to improve the health of my skin and gives me that fresh, natural glow that I love," Beckham said in a release. The designer and beauty icon has often commented on how health fats like avocado and salmon keep her skin plump and glowing, so it's a natural fit that she would develop a moisturizer that mimics that effect.

The moisturizer utilizes Bader's proprietary TFC8 technology to contribute to the skin’s own repair and renewal systems, so it's a primer with anti-aging and skincare benefits. "Infused with powerful actives, the Cell Rejuvenating Priming Moisturizer is clinically tested and shown to help support cell turnover and improve radiance, plump and tone skin, while immediately minimizing the appearance of pores and imperfections," notes the brand.

Victoria Beckham Beauty

One pump is enough for your whole face, but the glow it leaves behind is so pretty that you'll want to use two. It instantly brightens skin so that you'll feel like you can use less foundation, and it sinks into skin in seconds. Plus, we love that the formula is clean and it comes housed in a sleek glass tube with minimal plastic.

"It was an honor to collaborate with Victoria for her first skin launch. I am excited to share some of our skincare benefits in this product. It's the first priming moisturizer of its kind to care for your skin cells while also preparing your skin for makeup application," adds Bader.
