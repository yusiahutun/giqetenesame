Memes make fun of Oregonians that now have to pump own gas

Some states do things a little bit better than others, and Oregon used to be one of them.

But now, residents of the Beaver State might have to start pumping their own gas, just like the rest of us who don't live in New Jersey.

Thanks to a state law that went into effect on Monday, counties with 40,000 residents or less are now free to make their fuel pumps self-serve, all day, every day.

That's quite a change for a place where it used to be illegal to gas up on your own, and people are kind of freaking out about it.

The local CBS affiliate did its journalistic duty and documented some responses to the change, in a Facebook poll asking for public opinion on this very serious matter.

The responses were heated, humorous and helpful.

'Pumped my own gas my whole life and now my hand have literally melted down to my wrists,' he wrote.

'I'm typing this with my tongue.' 

Perhaps Mike Perrone read that account, before deciding he is simply not participating in the dangerous activity that is pumping his own gas.

This change in law isn't entirely new to rural counties in Oregon, though.

Legislation passed in 2015 that let the same gas station owners in low-population areas provide a self-serve option between the hours of 6.00 pm and 6.00 am.

The Culver Shell & Feed in Prineville took advantage of it back then, and made sure the pumps had payment options, owner Jeffrey Honeywell said.

That was a good thing for drivers in need late at night after gas station attendants had gone home for the evening, but not everyone opted in then, and not everyone is opting in now.

Some gas stations, like the Main Station Express in Prineville and the Metolius Market in Metolius, don't have the equipment to let customers pump and pay on their own, so their patrons won't be affected just yet, a manager and owner told KTVL.

That's lucky for those without the know-how, like the man who said a weekend course on proper pump use should be offered.

He's concerned about avoiding spraying toxic chemicals all over children, like he did last time he tried to pump his own gas.

Thankfully for him, Honeywell has promised to provide help for those that need it, and so have countless other good Samaritans, like Jim Bryant. 

He wrote: 

'FEAR NOT OREGON!!!! I've decided to move to Oregon to open a school to teach people how to pump their own gas.'

For those who think his entrepreneurial endeavor may be short-lived, Bryant had lots of ideas to expand his courses, to include things like 'tying your own shoes, counting past 10, and wiping your own butt.' 

Pumping your own gas can be a dangerous thing, as shown here in this meme

'I understand Oregon, adulting is hard and sometimes you someone to show you how,' he said. 'I'm here for you.'

For those who don't have the time to take a class, internet users provided a few more tips, in photo and text.

One image showed a woman pouring gas onto the exterior of her car, with the caption, 'I have no idea what I'm doing,' to show how not to fill up your own gas tank.

Another person named Des Mello responded to comments by women who feared gassing up while in a skirt.

The takeaway from that tip was that skirts may not be the best wardrobe choice in any especially windy evironment, whether you're getting gas or not, and that the nozzle goes in the car.

Nearly half of Oregons 36 counties are subject to the law change.
