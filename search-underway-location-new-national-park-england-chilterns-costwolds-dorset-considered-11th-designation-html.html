Search underway for location of NEW National Park for England with Chilterns, Cotswolds and Dorset a

A search is underway for a new national park in England to increase the amount of time people spend in nature.

Areas of outstanding national beauty (AONB) in the Chilterns, Cotswolds and combined regions of Dorset and east Devon are among those being considered by National England.

The decision is expected to be made quickly and the area will become the country's 11th official park, joining regions such as Dartmoor and the Peak District. However, designation could take years and the park will not be established before the General Election.

Environment Secretary Steve Barclay has presented National England with the task, following the Conservatives' 2019 manifesto promise to deliver new parks.

The Government is keen for more Britons to get out into the country's green areas, after figures from the Office for National Statistics showed the time we spend in the natural world has fallen since the pandemic.

National Parks are areas of relatively undeveloped and scenic landscape, where planning controls are a little more restrictive than elsewhere.

The last one created was the chalk grasslands of the South Downs in 2010.

In 2022, at the UN biodiversity summit in Montreal, the Government set a target to increase protected areas to 30 per cent of the land, which is likely to be boosted by the announcement of a new park.

The Cop28 climate summit starts in Dubai tomorrow and nature is one of the UK delegation's top five priorities.

The importance of the natural world to the British public was exemplified in September by the extensive condemnation towards the felling of the 50ft Sycamore Gap tree in Northumberland.

Rishi Sunak said: 'I shared in the nation's profound sense of anger in response to what happened at the Sycamore Gap earlier this year, but the public's outrage fundamentally demonstrated just how much love the British People have for the natural world.'
