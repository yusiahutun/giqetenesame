15 Ways to Wear Cowboy Boots

1

Old Hollywood Westerns

Getty Images

Actress Wendy Waldron pairs her cowboy boots with a bra top and high-waisted shorts circa 1949.

Marilyn Monroe

Getty Images

Bringing the boots from the '30s and '40s classic western movies into the 1950s, the iconic Hollywood actress poses for a Valentine's Day photo shoot.

3

Raquel Welch

Getty Images

The leading lady went full-on Americana in a patriotic swimsuit and white cowboy boots for the film, Myra Breckinridge—giving the classic boot a sexy upgrade.

Studio 54 Nights

In the early '80s, club-goers at New York's iconic Studio 54 embraced the trend in white with cascading dresses.

Pamela Anderson

Getty Images

Giving it the '90s grunge treatment, the blonde bombshell rocked a pair of black patent cowboy boots with denim cut-offs and a white tee.

Daisy Duke's Comeback

Getty Images

With the 2005 movie remake of Dukes of Hazzard, Jessica Simpson emerged as the new Daisy Duke: denim cut-offs, western belts, and a pair of bright red cowboy boots to top it all off.

The Early Aughts

The cowboy boot enjoyed a mini revival yet again in the early 2000s—this time gracing the red carpet. From Britney Spears to Sheryl Crow and even Rihanna, the boot made a trendy comeback (though the craze was slightly short-lived).

8

Taylor Swift

Getty Images

In 2008, country's breakout teenage star made every girl across America want to pair cowboy boots with sundresses.

Carrie Underwood

The country singer rocks her boots both on and off the stage—pairing them with everything from shorts to skirts and dresses.

Country Strong

In the 2010 film, Gwyneth Paltrow transformed into a country singer—cowboy boots, guitar, and all.

Kate Moss
