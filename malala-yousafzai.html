Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani girl education activist and the youngest Nobel Laureate ever. She is well-known for her human rights activism, particularly in the area of women’s and children’s education in her native Swat Valley, in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, northwest Pakistan, where the Taliban had previously prohibited girls from attending school. Desmond Tutu, an activist, nominated her for the International Children’s Peace Prize. She was awarded Pakistan’s first National Youth Peace Prize in 2012, as well as the 2013 Sakharov Prize. She and Shiza Shahid co-founded the Malala Fund, a non-profit organization, and co-authored the international bestseller I Am Malala in 2013.
