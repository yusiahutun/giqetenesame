2. Rolex’s ‘Lemon Paul Newman’ Daytona

There are few associations in horology as inseparable as Paul Newman and the Rolex Daytona, and this 18-carat gold chronograph with classic lemon dial is causing as much buzz today as it did when the piece first resurfaced in 2013.

Bling bust? Rolex is entering the second-hand market for the first time

3. Cartier’s Panthère de Cartier

No list of gold watches this year would be complete without the Panthère de Cartier, which saw new versions in rose gold and yellow gold. The luscious golden plum minimalist dial takes this classic design into the now.

4. Parmigiani Fleurier’s Tonda PF Skeleton rose gold

Contrasts do not get more elegant than this Tonda, which pairs a greyscale skeleton dial with solid rose gold everywhere else. Simple baton markers and skeleton hands make ideal accents for a piece where less is so much more.

5. Omega’s Speedmaster Moonwatch Professional Chronometer Chronograph

Omega evokes the golden age of space exploration in this iteration of the pioneering piece that went to the moon, with a polished 18-carat caseback engraved for good measure with the words, “First watch worn on the moon.”

6. Zenith’s Chronomaster Sport rose gold

The tricoloured dials stand out along with this racing watch’s solid rose gold bracelet, case and crown, harkening back to historic earlier versions of the piece when such a chronograph was a racer’s most essential and best friend.
