What Susan Lucci Eats In a Day

Iconic soap opera star Susan Lucci played the glamorous Erica Kane on All My Children for over 40 years. Today, the 71-year-old actress looks better than ever, sticking to a healthy diet and daily pilates routine to stay glowing and active. Here, Lucci invited BAZAAR.com into her home to see exactly what she cooks up for breakfast, lunch, and dinner, sharing some of her best-kept diet secrets along the way.

Pre-pilates, Lucci loves to start her day with a cup of coffee with organic sugar and whole milk, stating that she finds coffee "so personal" and that "everyone has to mix their own" to their liking. She'll then get her workout in before breakfast with a pilates routine that she started right before she went on Dancing with the Stars. She swears is the ultimate secret to giving her "core strength, flexibility and energy."

Breakfast needs to be "fuel she can run on," her go-to being Greek yogurt with organic pumpkin seeds and lingonberries. If it’s cheat day, she "wants it to be worth it," and will splurge on eggs or her husband's blueberry pancakes.

For lunch, she still wants to be "fit and feeling good," so will substitute bread with a base of beefsteak tomatoes. She then tops that off with slices of roasted turkey, avocado, salt & pepper, champagne vinegar, olive oil, and fresh mango.

However, Lucci does reveal that she wasn't always eating this healthy. "Before I was working out, if somebody told me that I would actually choose fruit over french fries, I would never believe them." Relatable, indeed.

Dinner is "date night" for her and husband, chef Helmut Huber. The couple cooks up a light salad to start, along with an entrée of salmon, broccoli, and steamed carrots. They also pair it with some champagne, as "it's always good to have a little bubbly."
