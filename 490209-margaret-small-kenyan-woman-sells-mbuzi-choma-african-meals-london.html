Margaret Small: Kenyan Woman Sells Mbuzi Choma, Other African Meals in London

  • Margaret Small is the proprietor of Kenya Kitchen which offers home-cooked Kenyan dishes in the heart of London, United Kingdom
  • The youthful 30-year-old woman revealed she learnt cooking skills from her grandmother in Funyula, Busia county
  • The restaurant has hosted Kenyans from all walks of life, including Kenya's High Commissioner to the UK Manoah Esipisu and National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula

When Margaret Small left Kenya 17 years ago in 2006, she did not imagine that she would one day own an exotic restaurant in London famed for its Kenyan dishes and other African meals.

The 30-year-old is the proprietor of Kenya Kitchen which offers home-cooked Kenyan dishes in the heart of London, United Kingdom.

According to Kenya Kitchen's website, the idea to cook Kenyan meals began back in 2018 in Margaret’s little kitchen at home.

"Food is an art. I'm passionate about cooking- the tradition of it, making it, and sharing it," Margaret said.

How Margaret learnt to cook

The youthful woman revealed she learnt cooking skills from her grandmother in Funyula, Busia county.

"At the age of six, I made my first meal of ugali and mrenda. It was then that I knew that I could cook," she narrated as reported by the Diaspora Messenger.

Her desire to popularise Kenyan food among UK residents birthed Kenya Kitchen. She opined that Kenya was known for its tourism and athletics but not its food. As such, she embarked on a journey to fill the gap.

"I opted to carve out this niche and tell the Kenyan story through food. I felt there was a gap in the market for Kenyan food, and there are so many cuisines. So, I opted to introduce Kenyan food to the world," she said.

Her restaurant located at Edgware sells mbuzi choma (roasted goat meat), mushenye (sweet potato mashed in beans), mokimo kienyeji, Kenya-style pilau, traditional style tilapia, ugali, sukuma wiki and other vegetables.

Margaret's father's role

Margaret's father, Kennedy Bwire, disclosed that she motivated his daughter to establish the restaurant after her cooking skills went viral.

Bwire who serves as Kenya Kitchen's accountant explained that she was invited by a person who saw her work online to cook and people loved her food.

"People suggested then that she start selling her food because they love it," he said.

The restaurant has hosted Kenyans from all walks of life. They include Kenya's High Commissioner to the UK Manoah Esipisu, National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetang'ula and a delegation of Kenyan lawmakers.
