Amount Bayern Munich expect to earn from Harry Kane shirts after signing star

Bayern Munich are reportedly expecting an eye-watering windfall of £100million from shirt sales thanks to Harry Kane's move to Bavaria.

Kane was confirmed as a Bayern player on Saturday morning - swapping North London for Germany to the tune of €100million (£86.4million). And he could be worth his weight in gold to the reigning Bundesliga champions.

According to the Mirror, Bayern are expecting to sell around one million shirts with Kane’s name and number on the back - worth a cool £100million. However, Bayern won't bank all that cash, with Adidas netting £52million-per-year, as part of their sponsorship deal.

However, the increased profits could be worth around £30million - paying back the Kane fee in less than three seasons. Bayern have been selling shirts at an increased rate, in recent seasons outselling Real Madrid and Manchester United.

Daniel Levy, who was at the centre of Tottenham's negotiations, is sad to lose Kane: "We sought over a long period of time to engage Harry and his representatives in several forms of contract extension, both short and long term.

"Harry was clear, however, that he wanted a fresh challenge and would not be signing a new contract this summer."
