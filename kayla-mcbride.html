Kayla McBride Wiki, Age, Height, Net Worth, Dating, Affairs &amp; Boyfriend

Kayla McBride is an American professional basketball player, well known as a shooting guard for the Las Vegas Aces of the Women’s National Basketball Association. She is also notable for her play for Notre Dame Fighting Irish women’s basketball team where she led the team to four consecutive Final Fours and three NCAA championship appearances.

Who Is Kayla McBride? Bio & Wiki

Kayla McBride was born on 25th June 1992, in Erie, Pennsylvania, United States. McBride is the daughter of LaMont (father) and LuAnn McBride (mother). She grew up with her younger brother, Aaron and two young sisters, Jayden and Karlee McBride who plays basketball for Indiana University.

McBride attended Villa Maria Academy located in Erie, Pennsylvania where she became a three-year starter. Furthermore, she also became a four-year letter winner under head coach, Scott Dibble. Later, McBride graduated from the University of Notre Dame where she played in 19 games. In the starting four games, McBride averaged 8.7 points, 3.3 rebounds, and shot 557 from the field, the third-highest on her team.

Kayla McBride’s Career

Kayla McBride started her career from the 2014 WNBA Draft where the San Antonio Stars drafted her as a shooting guard. While there, she scored in double-figures in 21 games, including two 30-point performances, while recording the most points by a Stars rookie (442) since the team moved to San Antonio.

Recently, McBride signed with UMMC Ekaterinburg of the Russian League for the 2018-2019 off-season. On 28th June 2018, she scored a new career-high of 38 points in a 97-91 loss to Dallas Wings where Skylar Diggins-Smith also played.

McBride got her big break after she became selected in the USA Basketball U18 team. There she played under the head coach, Jennifer Rizzotti in 2010. Later, she participated in the FIBA Americas U18 Championship For Women held at the U.S. Olympic Training Center in Colorado Springs, Colorado. Together, McBride won all five games, earning the gold medal for the event and a place in the 2011 U19 World Championship for women.
