List of top 10 most toxic fandoms in the world as of 2022

The internet has brought so much that it is hard to imagine how folks survived in the pre-internet era. However, that is just how the world operates – an invention comes up, people get hooked and hardly survive without it. One of the most outstanding advantages of the internet is connecting with various people from different parts of the world. And if you are looking for a platform with like-minded individuals who share similar interests, then a fandom can do. The culture has been on the rise, but, in some cases, it has turned out to be toxic. What are the most toxic fandoms in the world in 2022?

Don't let the face value of a fandom fool you; there is more to it than meets the eye. If you don't join the community, you will never know. Most people look for a platform where they can freely share their opinion without anyone scolding them. However, some fandoms have a deep and dark element.

10 most toxic fandoms in the globe in 2022

Fandoms are great platforms for people with the same interests to interact. However, some members can be rude and answer specific questions with disgust. And if this goes for a while, with the support of other members, then the fandom can be toxic.

Even though most fandoms don’t start as places of toxicity and hatred, there is a high potential of taking that direction. So what is the most toxic fandom in 2022?

1. Naruto

Naruto is a popular anime that finished airing years ago. However, its end hasn't stopped fans talking about it. Naruto has the most considerable number of toxic anime fans.

2. Barbz (Nicki Minaj fandom)

Don't dare comment about Nicki Minaj, as fans will come down on you like crazy if you don't know anything about Nicki Minaj. It is one of the most toxic fandoms in the world for newbies and anyone who has an opinion that others would consider defamatory to the famous musician.

The Barbz are fiercely loyal. A great example is when a writer for British GQ explained how Cardi B had adopted Nicki Minaj's style in a much more accessible way. She wrote:

Nicki Minaj intimidates, Cardi B endears.

Of course, this did not go well with Nicki Minaj fans, who responded with an all-out attack on the writer. The attacks were so toxic that some fans threatened to kill her. They even threatened that if she doesn't delete the post, they will hunt her and her family.

3. Swifties (Talyor Swift fandom)

Who knew that Taylor Swift would have a big fanbase of loyal fans that don't tolerate negative comments about her? The singer's fans are not bad, but they love her so much and are a bit over-the-top about it, making them extremely sensitive. Who can blame them? The pop star that sends them holiday presents.

Among this group of fans, some are toxic and complain about many things. For instance, they go on a rant when Taylor Swift doesn't get nominated for enough awards.

On the other hand, there are white supremacists. These fans call Taylor Swift an Aryan goddess and give her a bad reputation. They make the fandom one of the worst in the world.

4. Zack Snyder fans

Zack Snyder fans have had it rough, and their attitude tells it all. The star director occasionally blinks out and makes good and sometimes not-so-good movies, according to critics.

For instance, Dawn of the Dead and Watchmen were highly acclaimed. However, certain films like Sucker Punch, Man of Steel, and Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice have been trashed by critics. This hasn’t gone well with his fans that have been going online to express their discontent.

5. Rick and Morty fans

Rick and Morty is a funny, intelligent, and challenging cartoon. People have compared it with Futurama. The fandom, on the other hand, isn't so lovely as they are pretty toxic. Most fans boast how the show is so intelligent, and there is none comparable in its category.

6. Metalheads

Metalheads make the list of the most cancerous fandoms in the world. They are opinionated people with a superiority complex like no other. They have numerous obsessive qualities and are quick to aggression. Of course, not all are like this, but the majority bring out this toxic element.

In most cases, metalheads try to shove their taste on other people and have long arguments that go along the line, “Why don’t you listen to real music like metal?” “Aren’t you a metal fan?”

The worst thing is that metalheads fail to acknowledge the existence of other genres, which makes them hate. And the hatred is channelled to people that are hardly familiar with metal music.

7. Football fans

Football fans are crazy about the sport and, in most cases, only have harmless conversations with one another. However, a small percentage, likely 10% of the community, are toxic and make it one of the bad fandoms in the world.

The insults go as far as physically harming one another because of personal preferences. There have been instances in Europe and America where fans have killed or attacked each other because of conflicting opinions.

Some football clubs are infamous for racial slurs against certain players the accusers don't want in their club.

8. Nintendrones

Does Nintendo have the worst fanbase in the world? It could be as the fans are sometimes toxic to the core. The firm produces games and consoles and has an obnoxious fanbase.

Over the years, Nintendo has been making some wrong moves that have negatively affected their business model, but the fans remain docile to its poor decisions. Instead, they attack anyone that attempts to point out the company's faults.

Nintendo fans think of the company as the Holy Grail of gaming, and they insult other gamers for choosing other consoles or games.

BTS’ Army also qualifies as one of the most toxic fandoms in the world. However, the platform didn't start as harmful as it used to be a typical K-pop fandom. It was alright during the first 2-3 years after BTS debuted in 2013. After that, the fandom grew fast and became one of the biggest in the world, considering the massive global success the K-pop group has had over the years.

BTS fans are toxic because of how they treat people not affiliated with them and other K-pop groups. They lash out openly at anyone that dislikes or criticizes BTS. Some even go further and send insults and threats to specific individuals. They are good at rallying up a mob and going against a person, harassing them to the end.
