9 brilliant shows and movies coming to Netflix in June

There's Never Have I Ever, Black Mirror and more to look forward to

    Netflix has big plans for June! Welcoming a new roster of TV shows and films, the streaming platform is gifting us with some seriously binge-able content. From the return of popular series, including Black Mirror, and The Witcher, to original movies like and , here's what's on our must-watch list.    

    Netflix shows coming out in June

    Never Have I Ever

    Returning for its fourth and final season, will debut on Netflix on 8 June. Promising plenty of drama, the trailer has already teased a number of major milestones for Devi and her pals as they prepare for the prom, their high school graduation and college. 

    Black Mirror

    Back for a sixth instalment, is returning with a stellar cast. Aaron Paul, Annie Murphy, Ben Barnes, Salma Hayek and Joshua Hartnett are just some of the stars fronting the dystopian series. 

    Fans have been clued in on some of the season's storylines so far. Expect an unspeakable tragedy in space, an American couple who uncover a Scottish town's dark past, and a starlet dealing with the aftermath of a hit-and-run. 

    Our Planet II 

    Narrated by the national treasure that is David Attenborough, is scheduled for release on 14 June. The official synopsis reads: "At any given moment on planet Earth, billions of animals are on the move. Captured with spectacular and innovative cinematography, Our Planet II unravels the mysteries of how and why animals migrate to reveal some of the most dramatic and compelling stories in the natural world."

    The Witcher 

    Henry Cavill returns in his final outing as the White Wolf. Scheduled to be released in two parts, volume one of season three will premiere on 29 June. After Geralt decides to take Ciri into hiding, Yennefer guides them to the protected fortress of Aretuza but what unfolds is a "battlefield of political corruption, dark magic, and treachery." 

    The Days

    This new title is an eight-part drama that is being compared to HBO's Chernobyl by fans – so it's bound to be worth a watch! explores the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant accident which occurred in 2011 and focuses on different perspectives to tell the story. As the synopsis explains: "Blamed by some, hailed as heroes by others, those involved with Fukushima Daiichi face a deadly, invisible threat – an unprecedented nuclear disaster."

    Netflix films coming out in June

    A Beautiful Life

    is a brand-new Danish musical that has landed on the streaming platform. It stars Danish singer Christopher in the leading role as Elliot, a young artist who is discovered by a high-profile music industry manager named Suzanne, who recognizes his talent and wants to make him a star. However, Elliot's journey to international fame comes with its own challenges as he comes to terms with past struggles as well as his budding love for Suzanne's daughter, Lily.

    Extraction 2 

    Tyler Rake is back in action. After barely surviving the events of the first movie, the black ops mercenary is tasked with rescuing the family of a ruthless Georgian gangster from a prison. Chris Hemsworth, Olga Kurylenko, Daniel Bernhardt and Golshifteh Farahani star. 

    To Leslie 

    It's the film that got tangled up in Oscars drama. Garnering widespread critical acclaim, follows Leslie Rowland – a single mother and alcoholic who squanders all the money that she'd won in a lottery sweep. Film fans can watch it from 1 June. 

    The Perfect Find 

    Searching for a new romantic comedy? Gabrielle Union and Keith Powers have just the thing. Based on the book of the same name, follows Jenna Jones, a fashion editor who runs into problems after she learns that the stranger she kissed at a party is her new co-worker and the boss' son.
