Simeon Nyachae to be buried in a private ceremony on Monday, February 15

- The family of Simeon Nyachae confirmed the former Cabinet minister will be laid to rest at his Nyaribari home

- The burial ceremony will be preceded by a funeral service to be held at Nyanturago stadium on the same day February 15

- The ex-MP breathed his last at the Nairobi Hospital after a long battle with an undisclosed illness on Monday, February 1

The family of former Cabinet minister and 2002 presidential candidate Simeon Nyachae has set the burial date for the one-time powerful Abagusii kingpin.

Through his eldest son lawyer and East African Legislative Assembly MP Charles Nyachae, the family confirmed it will bury the late patriarch on Monday, February 15.

The 88-year-old retired politician will be laid to rest in a private ceremony attended by close family members at his Nyosia home in Nyaribari Chache, Kisii county.

The burial ceremony will be preceded by a funeral service to be held at Nyanturago stadium that will kick off from 9am after which he will be driven to his final resting place later in the day.

Nyachae's political life

Nyachae who led the Ford People party during his brighter political days breathed his last at the Nairobi Hospital after a long battle with an undisclosed illness on Monday, February 1.

He first won the Nyaribari Chache parliamentary seat in 1992 and held it until his retirement from active politics in 2007 when he failed to get re-elected.

In former president Daniel Moi's government, the deceased served as an Agriculture and Finance minister before resigning in 1999 after a nasty fall-out with the late head of state.

In 2002, his attempt to become Kenya's president hit a snag after then Narc's Mwai Kibaki floored him to become the third head of state.
