'Succession' sweeps the board at the 2024 Golden Globes

If there was one TV show that truly dominated our screens last year, it was Succession, which aired its final episode in May and left us all in mourning for Waystar Royco and ludicrously capacious bags. At the Golden Globes, however, we were treated to a cast reunion, in honour of the series' whopping nine nominations – including Best Television Series - Drama, and performance nods for each of the warring members of the Roy family.

ROBYN BECK//Getty Images

The Succession cast reunited on the red carpet at the Golden Globes

The stars all arrived separately on the red carpet, but got together for a few full-cast shots. J. Smith-Cameron attended the ceremony in a royal-blue Christian Siriano gown, which she paired with diamond starburst earrings, a gold clutch and a simple, low bun. Alan Ruck also made his return to the red carpet for the Golden Globes, just two months after a car accident in which he injured his leg. He was joined at the event by his wife, The Killing actress Mireille Enos. Sarah Snook, meanwhile chose a black lace gown by Prabal Gurung, which comprised a semi-sheer top and a full tulle skirt, and accessorised with diamond drop earrings by Neil Lane Couture.

Snook scored her second Golden Globe – Best Actress, for the role of Shiv Roy – after previously taking home the award for Best Supporting Actress in 2022 for the show's third season. During her speech, she thanked the show's cast and crew as well as her family, giggling at one point as she said the series had "changed my life! Everybody in it is amazing, the cast and crew are fantastic. This is a team effort. This was always a team and that was what made the show amazing to be a part of."

She was far from the only Succession star to take home a gong – Kieran Culkin accepted the award for Best Actor in a Drama Series with a heart-warming speech. "I was nominated for a Golden Globe like 20 years ago, and when that moment passed, I remember thinking, 'I'm never gonna be back in this room again.' Which, was fine, whatever," he said. "Thanks to Succession, I've been in here a couple times. It's nice but I sort of accepted I was never gonna be on the stage, so this is a nice moment."

Michael Buckner//Getty Images

Kevin Mazur//Getty Images

Kieran Culkin with his wife, Jazz Charton

He then briefly paused to acknowledge fellow nominee Pedro Pascal, jokingly telling the actor, "Suck it, Pedro. Mine!" as the camera quickly panned to show the Last of Us actor laughing along and quickly pretending to cry. Culkin walked the carpet alongside his wife Jazz Charton, with the couple co-ordinating in all-black looks. Culkin accessorised his Dior suit with stacks of colourful beaded bracelets, courtesy of his children.

Matthew Macfadyen also won his first-ever Golden Globe, for Best Supporting Actor, and said in his speech that he "just adored every second playing with the wonderful human grease stain that is Tom Wambsgans".

CBS Photo Archive//Getty Images

Matthew Macfayden accepting his Golden Globe

For Succession's fourth and final award of the night, the entire cast joined creator Jesse Armstrong onstage to accept the gong for Best Television Series - Drama. In his speech, Armstrong shouted out the show's "subtle and supple cast" and also acknowledged the "bittersweet" decision to end the series on season four.

Rich Polk//Getty Images

Jesse Armstrong acknowledged the "bittersweet" end of the series

ROBYN BECK//Getty Images

Macfayden, Snook and Culkin laughing together on the red carpet

This marks the series' third Globe in the category, following its victories at the 2020 and 2022 ceremonies – tying it with The X-Files (1994, 1996 and 1997) and Mad Men (2007-09) for the most wins for best drama at the Golden Globes.

It was a night that made history for the show – and after all, what else would we expect from TV's most cutthroat family?
