So, which exotic cats can you legally keep in your home?

Exotic cat breeds you can legally keep as pets

Before buying or bringing rare exotic cat breeds into your home as pets, understand the traits of each breed. It will help you determine whether you can legally keep the pet in your home or whether it will best suit your family. Here are 15 exotic cat you can lawfully keep as pets in your home.

1. American Bobtail

The American Bobtail is a moderately long and substantial cat with a rectangular stance and one of the best legal exotic cats to keep as a pet. It has a full and broad chest with slightly higher hips and prominent shoulder blades.

The cat is notable for its stubby bobbed tail and wild and rugged appearance. However, the American Bobtail is the best to keep at home because of its gentle disposition and playful personality.

2. American Shorthair

The American Shorthair is a breed of domestic cat believed to have descended from European cats brought to North America by early settlers to protect their crops and homes.

It is a stocky, muscular cat with a short, dense coat donned in various colours, and patterns. American Shorthairs are good exotic pets because of their intelligence, affectionate personalities, low maintenance, and long lifespans.

3. Bengal

The Bengal is a hybrid of the Asian leopard cat and a domestic cat. They have a beautiful spotted or marbled coat pattern with an orange hue and an athletic build.

Bengals can make great family cats because they are playful. Also, their large size makes them active cats. And despite having a rugged appearance, Bengals are intelligent, sweet, friendly, and loving.

4. British Shorthair

The British Shorthair is the purebred version of the traditional British domestic cat and one of the oldest recognised exotic cat breeds. It has a stocky body, dense coat, and broad face and weighs between 7 and 17 pounds, making it easy to identify large exotic cats as pets.

British Shorthairs are good family pets because they are loyal, alert, and playful. Although they are not overly active, they are also not destructive.

5. Lykoi

The Lykoi is a breed of rare, partially hairless cat that looks like a werewolf. They have sparse and often patchy fur on their bodies, with a roan pattern of white and coloured hairs and a distinctive facial structure.

Lykois are exotic cats with slender bodies but have large ears and bright eyes. Lykois are social cats because they are interactive, friendly, and love meeting new people.

6. Maine Coon

The Maine Coon is the largest domesticated cat breed in the world. The exotic cat typically weighs between 9 and 18 pounds. Maine Coons have a thick, luxurious double-long hair coat and a robust and muscular body.

They are also known to be exceptionally social, even with strangers and can remain playful into old age. Additionally, the cats are intelligent, affectionate, and gentle.

7. Munchkin

The Munchkin is one of the best exotic cats to own. It is a medium-sized exotic cat breed characterised by short legs caused by a genetic mutation.

Munchkins are friendly and curious breeds with thick plush coats in various colours and patterns. Also, they are active cats that enjoy exploring, which makes them proficient hunters. The cats also like playing, including stealing small, shiny objects.

8. Oriental Shorthair

The Oriental Shorthair is one of the many cat breeds that are considered exotic by pet enthusiasts. The cat is a breed of domestic cat developed from and closely related to the Siamese cat.

Oriental shorthairs are known to be intelligent and curious cats that love to explore and play. They are also active cats and require regular physical and mental stimulation to stay healthy and happy.

9. Pixie-bob

The Pixie-bob is a breed of cats with a wild-looking coat and a bobcat-like face. It is also a large cat with a stocky and robust build, strikingly resembling the North American bobcat.

Pixie-bobs are known for their intelligence and loyalty. Moreover, it is easy to leash-train a Pixie-bob and make the exotic cat interact with other strangers.

10. Russian Blue

The Russian Blue is a medium-sized cat with a thick, silvery-blue coat that can also range in colour from light shimmering silver to a darker, slate grey. Also, the cat has a distinctive triangular head and wide ears.

Russian Blues are known for their intelligence, loyalty, and sweet-tempered personality. They are also playful and make great companions for their owners.

11. Savannah

The Savannah is a hybrid between a wild African Serval and a domestic cat. It is one of the best domesticated wild cat breeds because of its high intelligence, wild appearance, energy, and outgoing personality. Physically, Savannahs are tall and lean with large ears and a spotted or marbled coat.

12. Scottish Fold

The Scottish Fold is a breed of cats with ears that fold forward and down, giving them a distinctive look. Also, Scottish Folds are medium-sized cats with round heads, large round eyes, and short, dense coats. They are known for their low maintenance, calm, sweet personalities and for being exceptionally loyal to their owners.

13. Siamese

The Siamese is a breed of domestic cat that originated in Thailand and is one of the oldest and most popular domesticated cat breeds. They have a unique pointed coat pattern, striking colour, and long, slim bodies.

Siamese cats have vocal personalities and can form strong bonds with their owners. The cats are also active and require plenty of exercise and stimulation.

14. Sphynx

The Sphynx is a hairless breed of cats with wrinkled, leathery skin. Despite their hairlessness, the breed is a medium-sized cat that weighs between six and twelve pounds. Also, the Sphynx is an affectionate and friendly exotic cat breed with plenty of intelligence and playful personalities.

15. Toyger

The Toyger is a medium-sized cat with a striped coat resembling a tiger. It is also an exotic cat breed with long, muscular bodies, round heads, and high shoulders. Toygers are well known for being intelligent. They are also active cats that love to play around and explore.

  • Can exotic cat breeds get sick? Exotic cat breeds can get sick and suffer from feline infectious peritonitis (FIP), polycystic kidney disease (PKD), and dental issues.
  • Which is the oldest exotic cat breed? The oldest exotic cat breeds are The Near Eastern Wildcat, Egyptian Mau, Abyssinian, and Japanese Bobtail.
  • What is the average weight of an exotic cat breed? The average weight of exotic cat breeds is between 4-7 kilograms.
  • Do exotic cat breeds eat a lot? Exotic cat breeds can vary in their eating habits, but they should not eat more than their body needs.
