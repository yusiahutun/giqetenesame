Who is Chuck Todd?

Chuck Todd is a famous American journalist and the 12th and current moderator of NBC’s Meet the Press. Before becoming a host of Meet the Press, Todd was the Chief White House correspondent for NBC and the host of The Daily Rundown on MSNBC.

Chuck was born on April 8, 1972, in Miami, Florida, to Lois Cheri (née Bernstein) and Stephen Randolph Todd. This means that the journalist will be turning 51 years old in 2023. He is Jewish from his mother's side and was raised Jewish.

The television host attended George Washington University, where he majored in political science and took a minor in music. However, he did not earn a degree.

Chuck's career started in the political field, where he did reports and analyses. For instance, Todd worked for the 1992 presidential campaign for Senator Tom Harkin. From 1992 to 2007, Chuck worked for National Journal's The Hotline, where he was the editor-in-chief.

Todd joined NBC in March 2007 and became a political director. Todd became the host of Meeting the Press on NBC on August 14, 2014 (where Luke Russert left), a role he holds until today.

On top of being a journalist, Todd is an adjunct professor at John Hopkins University. He is also the author of The Stranger: Barack Obama in the White House, published in 2014.

Chuck Todd lives in Arlington, Virginia, with his family, including his wife, Kristian Denny Todd and their two children. They tied the knot in 2001.

The controversial Chuck Todd weight loss experience

You have likely Chuck Todd’s weight loss and have wondered what happened. As the television reporter explained to Runner's World during an interview posted in January 2017, he made a significant lifestyle change that involved committing to exercise. Therefore, the weight loss he has been experiencing is attributable to his commitment to exercise.

The summer of 2013 was the unhealthiest I’d ever felt. I was close to 200 pounds, which, at five-foot-ten, is overweight. The stress of the job and middle age brought it on. So, I said, Lemme see if I can run for a week straight. Then it was two weeks, then three. I created a routine. By December, I'd dropped a few pounds. It was a real epiphany: Any day I didn't run, I felt crappy.

Chuck started running in the summer of 2013 and ran for a week straight. He adjusted to the routine and found himself running every day. According to him, by December 2013, he had dropped a few pounds and felt better on days he ran.
