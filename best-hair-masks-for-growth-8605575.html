The 10 Best Hair Masks for Growth of 2024, Tested

If there’s one beauty dream that’s popping up all over 2024 vision boards, it’s hair: Longer, fuller, healthier, stronger — we want it all. But getting your hair to grow faster and healthier is harder than it sounds, and you'll need specific products with specific ingredients to boost your efforts. “Hair masks can contribute to overall hair health by addressing factors that can influence hair growth,” says Dr. Isfahan Chambers-Harris, a Presidential STEM Fellow at the National Institute of Health, and trichologist. Most notably, these hair masks specifically developed for growth, she says, “can revive and support a healthy scalp, nourish strands, and prevent future damage, and these are all factors that contribute to an environment beneficial to hair growth.”

Is there such a thing as having hair that looks too voluminous or too healthy? We think not, and over six weeks, a team of InStyle editors tested 15 popular hair masks for hair growth in search of the very best. We judged each one on the immediate and long-term benefits and visible results they delivered directly to our strands: Improved hydration and hair strength, healthy hair that feels touchable and soft, and most importantly, if our hair was noticeably thicker, fuller, healthier at the end of the six-week test. These are the 10 hair masks that InStyle editors — and their newfound bouncy ‘90s supermodel hair — loved the most.

Although the brand recommends letting the mask soak into your hair for 30 minutes, we tapped out around 20 minutes just because we ran out of time, but the sweet, fruity scent made the entire experience feel so lovely.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Strengthening Ingredients: “Several ingredients are believed to be beneficial for strengthening hair and promoting hair growth,” says Dr. Chambers-Harris, most commonly, proteins like keratin, biotin, and collagen. Vitamin A, while not a protein. is a mighty strengthener to help prevent split ends and breakage.

    “Niacin, or vitamin B3, improves blood flow to the hair follicles, improving nutrient and oxygen delivery to the hair follicles, which promotes faster hair growth,” explains Chambers-Harris, adding that some ingredients are better for specific hair types than others. "For curly or coily hair, hydrating ingredients like argan oil or panthenol can provide moisture, strength and enhance curl definition, and thin or fine hair benefits from zinc, grapeseed extract, and B vitamins."

  • Protein-Packed Formula: A trio of proteins — keratin, biotin, and collagen — are called the building blocks of healthy hair, meaning you can find them in almost every strengthening or hair growth mask. “Keratin is a fibrous protein that is a key structural component of hair, nails, and the outer layer of the skin, and it repairs damage by filling in gaps and cracks within each hair strand,” explains Chambers-Harris, adding that biotin, or vitamin B7, is known to reduce hair thinning while also preventing dryness within hair or scalp. 

    “Collagen is responsible for hair’s elasticity and bounce, which translates into less breakage, fewer split ends, and staving off future damage, all of which work to preserve hair structure, leading to more growth.

  • Plant, Seed, or Fruit-Based Oils: “Nourishing and hydrating the hair are essential for maintaining overall hair health because they help address various factors that contribute to the strength, appearance, and resilience of the hair,” says Chambers-Harris.

    “If hair is over processed, dry and unhealthy, it simply will not grow as well as it normally would,” says Martin, adding that this is especially true of people who overdo it with daily heat styling tools. "Using masks and other hair products that add hydration and nourishment to the hair replaces the protein, keratin, and moisture that we strip out doing all of those things to support growth.”

    Plant-based and seed extracts contain vitamins, antioxidants, and minerals to ramp up hair health. Look for Martin's favorites, rosemary and lavender oils, along with Chamber-Harris's go-tos, coconut oil, jojoba oil, and sweet almond oil. “Coconut oil penetrates the hair shaft, providing deep conditioning and has antimicrobial properties that can benefit scalp health," says Dr. Chambers-Harris, adding that jojoba oil helps control excess sebum that weighs down hair and sweet almond oil is the cherry on top for a shiny finish.

  • Scalp Health: “A healthy scalp provides a supportive environment for hair follicles, ensuring optimal conditions for hair to grow,” says Chambers-Harris. Regular cleansing, a healthy, balanced diet, and avoid products with harsh chemicals will help keep your scalp healthy.

    “Sometimes products, especially dry shampoo and hairspray, build up on the scalp causing blockages at the follicle,” says Martin, who says the easy fix is simply brushing your hair before hopping in the shower to loosen build-up. “Use a clarifying shampoo once every few weeks to give the scalp a deep clean."

    Flakey scalps should kick it up a notch and look for specific ingredients, like anti-inflammatory aloe vera, tingly peppermint oil that brings more blood flow to the scalp, and moisturizing, antibacterial honey, to wipe them out and create a base that is primed and ready for healthy hair growth.

Your Questions, Answered

Can hair masks help hair growth?

“Hair masks can contribute to overall hair health and may indirectly support hair growth by addressing factors that can influence the hair growth,” says Chambers-Harris. “Most hair masks do not directly stimulate new hair follicle formation, but they can promote a healthy scalp, nourish the hair strands, and prevent damage, and these are all factors that contribute to an environment beneficial to hair growth.”

Hair masks work alongside a healthy diet, other quality hair products, and gentle hair care practices to help facilitate hair growth.

What mask makes hair grow faster?

While there is no mask that can single-handedly make your hair grow faster, they do whip unhealthy hair into shape, because no hair will grow quickly on a backed-up scalp with clogged hair follicles or brittle ends. "Masks can help hair growth by strengthening and fortifying the hair and keeping it healthy for longer,” says Martin. 

Even with this list of the best hair masks for growth, it's not an instant gratification kind of thing. Be patient and trust the process; if you stay committed, you will see results.

What improves hair thickness?

“Ultimately, hair thickness is largely based on your genetics,” says Martin, but with a healthy protein-packed diet and playing it safe with heat tools, you can do your part to keep hair from thinning.
